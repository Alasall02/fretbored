Technique Library
Step-by-step guides for every skill in your plan
1
Find the bend note
G string, 7th fret. Use your ring finger. Place middle and index behind it — all three push together.
2
Push toward the ceiling
Rotate your wrist (not just fingers) to push the string upward. A whole-step bend raises the pitch by 2 frets.
3
Hold and release slowly
Bend to peak, hold 2 seconds, then release slowly. That slow release is the emotional core of blues and Maggot Brain.
4
Practice target
10 clean bends, then add one to the end of a pentatonic phrase. The bend should feel like a sentence ending with a question.
1
Fret any note
Use your ring finger on B or high e string. Support with middle and index behind it.
2
Micro-bend oscillation
Push slightly sharp, release back to pitch, sharp again. Think of a singer wavering on a held note — that IS vibrato.
3
Wrist drives it
Your wrist rotation powers vibrato, not your fingers. Think of turning a doorknob back and forth quickly.
4
Control the speed
Slow vibrato = vocal and expressive. Fast = urgency. Learn slow first. Eddie Hazel uses slow wide vibrato on Maggot Brain.
1
Right hand position
Thumb (p) → E, A, D strings. Index (i) → G. Middle (m) → B. Ring (a) → high e.
2
Basic pattern
Thumb → Index → Middle → Ring → Middle → Index. Written: p-i-m-a-m-i. Hold G chord and loop this until automatic.
3
Thumb is the anchor
Thumb always hits the root note of each chord. G = 6th string, C = 5th string, D = 4th string.
4
This is Banana Pancakes
This exact pattern over G → C → Am → D is the intro and verse of Banana Pancakes. You're learning both simultaneously.
1
What is a montuno
Short, percussive, off-beat strums that drive salsa. Choppy and tight, not smooth.
2
The rhythm
Count: 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 +. Strum on the "and" of beats 2 and 3. Downbeats are often silent — off-beats are the accent.
3
Mute between strums
After each strum, rest your strumming hand on the strings to mute them. The mute IS part of the sound.
4
Practice over clave
Open Tracks → Salsa Clave. Start at 100 BPM. Feel your guitar lock with the clave. Then raise to 120.
1
The core progression
Am – G – F – E. Every classic bachata is built on this. The E chord creates tension that wants to resolve to Am — that longing IS bachata.
2
The strum pattern
Down–Down–Down–Down–Up with accent on beat 4. There's a tiny hesitation before the up strum — that rhythmic hiccup is the feel.
3
Two beats per chord
Am (2 beats) → G (2) → F (2) → E (2). Once automatic, try 4 beats per chord for a slower, more spacious feel.
4
Over a drum track
Open Tracks → Bachata Drum Track. Start at 90 BPM. Lock to the percussion. Raise to 100, then 120.
1
What is a power chord
Just 2 notes: root + 5th. No major or minor quality — works over everything. Foundation of rock and funk.
2
The shape
Index on root note (E or A string). Ring finger 2 frets up, 1 string down. That's it.
3
Move it anywhere
Same shape everywhere. Index on 5th fret E string = A5. 7th fret = B5. The note follows your index finger.
4
Add gain
Turn up the gain slightly on your electric. Strum just the 2 fretted strings. Palm muting adds a tight, punchy sound.
